What makes it distinct

The core fantasy

Sustainability, not sprawl

This isn't a city-builder. You never “win” by paving the map — growth and the living island have to rise together. The goal is prosperity the water, soil, forest and climate can actually sustain: a genuinely fresh answer to the build-forever strategy game.

Unique selling point

The land is the interface

Every choice is reflected on a living 3D island — forests thicken, rivers run, mines scar the ground, weather and a day/night cycle roll through. Your game state is your key art.

Unique selling point

A mandate, not a score screen

You don't lose on a stat tripwire. You serve a fixed term and face a council review that renews — or ends — your stewardship based on four factions' trust. Consequence is political.

Unique selling point

A living web of consequence

In the spirit of governance sims like Democracy, eight vitals, four factions and a real economy all feed each other in one connected model — only trade-offs you can see ripple across the island and trace node by node on the Systems Map.

The core loop

Read the island

Eight vital systems, four factions, a real budget — all legible on the diorama and dashboard.

Act within limits

Spend limited quarterly actions: place builds, pass sponsored bills, negotiate with the council.

Resolve the crisis

Risk-driven events, weighted by your actual pressures, each a visible trade-off.

Simulate the season

Watch the world advance — growth, drift, weather, consequences ripple outward.

Face the five-year review

The council weighs faction trust and decides whether your mandate holds.

Core mechanics

Vitals ×8

Water, Energy, Food, Soil, Biodiversity, Economy, Equity, Climate — held in constant tension.

Factions ×4

Farmers, Corporations, Ecologists, Indigenous council — each with grievances and a renewal vote.

Building

Free-form placement with painted valid zones, influence auras and adjacency synergies — forests feed farms, mines poison neighbours.

Policy

Faction-sponsored bills each quarter — a Democracy-style web where every choice earns trust somewhere and spends it elsewhere.

Economy

Quarterly credit, taxes, fluctuating export demand, upkeep, shortages and debt with interest.

Crises

A dozen rural-disaster archetypes, weighted by your real pressures and resolved through trade-offs.

Living world

Weather, day/night, wildlife — and night fireflies whose count is a living proxy for ecological health.

Finale

Journey Replay rewinds the whole run on the diorama with charts and story beats — endlessly shareable.
